Inside the report

A clear record of the valuation

The exact contents depend on the vehicle and assignment. The report brings the relevant evidence into one place.

Section 01

Claim and vehicle details

The report records the vehicle's year, make, model, trim, mileage, equipment, condition, and relevant claim information.

Section 02

Comparable vehicle review

Comparable listings and other market evidence are identified, sourced, and reviewed for location, timing, trim, mileage, and equipment.

Section 03

Value analysis and notes

Adjustments, source notes, limitations, and the appraiser's independent opinion are explained so the reader can follow the analysis.

This walkthrough is for format and educational purposes. It does not show a specific customer's claim, settlement, or expected result.

Why this works

Insurers respond to documentation, not frustration

Documented, not self-reported

A clear, organized vehicle value report can make it easier to show an insurer where its comparison vehicles or adjustments missed key details.

Built on their own rules

We document errors against the insurer's own methodology and your state's claim-handling standards - the language adjusters answer to.

Escalation-ready

Every report is prepared as if it may be used in an appraisal-clause proceeding or regulator complaint. Adjusters can tell.
