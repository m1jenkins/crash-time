How It Works

Three steps between you and a fair settlement.

You don't need to become an appraisal expert or spend weeks fighting on the phone. You gather what you already have; we do the heavy lifting.

Your part is simple

The customer journey

1

Send us your claim

Complete our short intake form and upload what you have: the insurer's offer or valuation report, repair estimates, photos, and your VIN. Missing something? We'll tell you exactly what to request from your adjuster. Takes about 10 minutes.

2

We build your independent report

A certified appraiser — never an algorithm, never a cookie-cutter template — personally looks at your vehicle's details, audits the insurer's numbers, and builds your valuation from comparable dealership listings, recent auction results, and private-party marketplace sales. Everything is documented to professional appraisal standards. Your report arrives by email within 48 hours.

3

Present it and negotiate

Every report includes ready-to-send correspondence templates and a step-by-step negotiation guide. Want backup? Add our advocacy service and we'll handle the adjuster conversations with you or for you.

Inside your report

What's in the report — chapter by chapter

Adjusters take a report seriously when it's built the way their own appraisers build them. Every CrashTime report follows the same three-chapter structure.

Chapter01

Claim & Damage Audit

We take the insurer's valuation apart line by line: the comparable vehicles they chose (and the ones they conveniently ignored), condition adjustments, mileage deductions, and repair-quality findings. Every error and omission is flagged and documented, so you know exactly where the offer went wrong.

Chapter02

Independent Market Valuation

Our own valuation of your vehicle, built by hand — not pulled from a single database — from comparable dealership listings, recent auction results, and private-party marketplace sales in your region, with options and trim corrections, and — for repaired vehicles — a documented diminished value calculation. This chapter is the number you negotiate from, with the methodology to defend it.

Chapter03

Correspondence & Evidence Pack

Everything you need to act: a ready-to-send demand letter citing your report, response templates for common adjuster pushback, your state's relevant claim-handling requirements, and a checklist for escalating to a supervisor, appraisal clause, or department of insurance complaint if the insurer won't move.

Common questions

What happens after you send the report?

Most claims: the insurer responds

A documented, credentialed counter-valuation changes the conversation. Many adjusters revise their offer once real evidence is on the table.

If they push back

Your Evidence Pack covers the common objections. If you'd rather not handle it alone, our hourly advocacy service takes over the negotiation — with fees capped so they never eat your recovery.

If it needs to escalate

Your policy's appraisal clause and your state's complaint process are powerful tools. We'll show you how to use them — and when it's time to talk to an attorney, we'll say so honestly.
