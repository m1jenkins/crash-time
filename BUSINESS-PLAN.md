# Totaled Texas — Business Plan

**Independent Total-Loss Appraisals · Certified Vehicle Valuations for Consumers**

> Working draft. Figures marked *(illustrative)* are planning assumptions to be
> validated against real pipeline data in the first two quarters of operation.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Totaled Texas is a founder-led, expert-driven service that helps consumers challenge
low total-loss insurance settlements. The core product is an **independent, certified
vehicle valuation report** — professionally documented evidence a consumer can present
to their insurer when the offered actual cash value will not replace the vehicle.

The business is built on an asymmetry: insurers value vehicles with automated
software optimized for fast, low settlements, while consumers typically have no
counter-evidence and no idea what their claim is worth. A credentialed appraiser's
report — packaged from $99 to $449, delivered in 48 hours, and backed by a money-back
guarantee — routinely changes settlement outcomes by thousands of dollars.

**Model in one line:** free expert claim review (lead magnet) → Essential, Guided,
or Specialty certified report → 24-hour priority and Specialty + Guided checkout
upsells → white-label/bulk reports for attorneys, credit unions, and fleets (B2B).

## 2. The Problem

- After a crash, insurers present total-loss valuations generated by proprietary
  software (e.g., comparable-vehicle databases
  with aggressive condition adjustments). The first offer is frequently below
  true local replacement cost.
- The consumer's alternatives are poor: accept the offer, hire an attorney
  (uneconomical for most vehicle-only claims), or argue without evidence.
- The emotional context matters: the customer has just been through a crash, may
  be without a car, and feels powerless against a large institution. They don't
  want a lecture on appraisal methodology — they want an advocate.

## 3. The Solution

A three-chapter certified appraisal report, produced by a credentialed independent
appraiser in 48 hours:

1. **Claim & Damage Audit** — line-by-line review of the insurer's valuation:
   comparable selection, condition/mileage adjustments, trim and options errors.
2. **Independent Market Valuation** — a defensible replacement value or diminished
   value figure built from verified market comparables and documented methodology.
3. **Correspondence & Evidence Pack** — demand letter, rebuttal templates,
   state claim-handling references, and an escalation checklist (supervisor →
   appraisal clause → department of insurance complaint).

Guided document support personalizes the submission package without negotiating or
communicating with the insurer. Specialty research adds VIN/build-sheet validation,
upgrade documentation, niche comparables, and a specialty-value appendix. Customers
may combine both for $449 or add 24-hour priority delivery for $49.

## 4. Market

- **Primary market:** US consumers with private-passenger total-loss claims. The US
  sees roughly 5–6 million police-reported
  crashes annually; total-loss frequency has trended near 20%+ of collision
  claims *(validate against current industry data)*.
- **Serviceable niche:** claimants whose vehicles are worth $8,000+ (enough value
  at stake for a $99–$449 report package to pay for itself several times over) and who
  are motivated to push back — realistically a low-single-digit percentage of
  eligible claims, which still implies a market of hundreds of thousands of
  cases/year.
- **Geography:** launch as a single-state/regional service (appraiser licensing is
  state-specific), then expand state-by-state.
- **B2B market:** personal injury attorneys (property-damage support for their clients),
  credit unions (protecting members' collateral value), and fleet operators.

## 5. Competition & Positioning

| Alternative | Weakness Totaled Texas exploits |
|---|---|
| Accepting the insurer's offer | No independent evidence; our free tier makes checking costless |
| DIY negotiation with internet printouts | No credentials, no methodology; adjusters dismiss it |
| Public adjusters | Focused on property/homeowner claims; % fees on the whole claim |
| Attorneys | Uneconomical for vehicle-only disputes; slow |
| National report mills | Templated, algorithmic reports; no careful audit, specialty research, or local market depth |

**Positioning:** *the credentialed ex-insider on your side.* Founder-led authority
(18 years, both sides of the table), single-niche specialization, radical
price transparency, a money-back guarantee, and an "if your offer is fair, we'll
tell you" honesty promise that converts trust into referrals.

**Brand value word:** *fairness* — used consistently in the hero, CTAs, and values.

## 6. Services & Pricing

| Tier | Price | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Free Claim Snapshot** | $0 | Lead magnet. Expert opinion (fair / borderline / low) + recoverable range, by email in 2 business days. No payment info collected. |
| **Essential Total Loss Report** | $99 | Complete standard report: certified appraisal, line-by-line audit, verified comparables, corrections, general templates, and 48-hour delivery. |
| **Guided Total Loss Report** | $299 | Essential plus claim-specific submission documents, evidence index, key-findings summary, a 30-minute walkthrough, and one factual addendum requested within 14 days. |
| **Specialty Total Loss Report** | $299 | Essential plus VIN/build-sheet research, upgrade documentation, niche comparables, rare-option analysis, and specialty-value appendix. |
| **Specialty + Guided** | $449 | Combines both $299 packages while charging the shared base report once. |
| **24-Hour Priority Delivery** | $49 add-on | Optional checkout add-on for every package; refunded if the priority deadline is missed. |
| **Partner Program (B2B)** | Volume pricing from 5 reports/mo | White-label reports, priority SLAs, or per-case referral fees for shops, attorneys, credit unions, fleets. |

**Guarantee mechanics:** if the independent appraisal does not support a value above
the insurer's offer, the report fee is refunded in full. This is affordable because
the free Snapshot pre-qualifies cases — clearly fair offers are filtered out before
anyone pays.

## 7. Revenue Model & Unit Economics *(illustrative)*

Planning assumptions to validate in the first 6 months:

- Free Snapshot → paid report conversion: **25–35%** (pre-qualified, high-intent leads).
- Paid report mix: ~40% Essential / 30% Guided / 30% Specialty → **blended ~$219/report**.
- Priority attach rate: **25%** at $49; Guided attach on Specialty: **25%** at $150.
- Target realized average order value: **$230–$245** before refunds and taxes.
- Variable cost per report: appraiser time (2.5–3.5 hrs), market data subscriptions,
  payment processing — target **60%+ gross margin** at founder-does-the-work stage.
- Refund rate under guarantee: target **<5%** (Snapshot filtering is the control).

Monthly steady-state sketch (founder + 1 part-time appraiser):

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Free Snapshots delivered | 120 |
| Paid reports (30% conv.) | 36 |
| Package revenue (36 × ~$219) | ~$7,884 |
| Priority add-ons (9 × $49) | $441 |
| Guided-on-Specialty add-ons (3 × $150) | $450 |
| B2B/white-label (10 reports × $175) | $1,750 |
| **Monthly revenue** | **~$10,525** |

Scale levers: additional certified appraisers (capacity), state expansion (market),
B2B pipeline (volume without CAC), and productized templates (margin).

## 8. Go-to-Market

1. **SEO content engine (primary).** The niche is question-rich and underserved:
   "total loss appraisal [state]", "total loss offer too low", "how to dispute
   insurance car value". Publish state-specific guides and calculators; the Free
   Snapshot is the conversion path on every page.
2. **Free tier as flywheel.** The Snapshot costs ~20 minutes of expert time and
   creates reciprocity, a qualified pipeline, and testimonial volume.
3. **B2B referral channels.** Body shops hand a "your car may have lost value —
   free check" card to every completed repair; attorneys and credit unions get
   white-label or referral terms. One good shop relationship ≈ steady monthly cases.
4. **PR / authority building.** Local TV consumer desks, auto podcasts, and regional
   business press love the "ex-insurance appraiser switches sides" story. Each
   feature feeds the trust bar. *(Trust bar ships with placeholders — populate only
   with real features.)*
5. **Community workshops** ("Know Your Claim") — genuine goodwill, local press
   hooks, and grassroots referrals, tied to the giving-back program.
6. **Paid search (later).** High-intent keywords once conversion rates are known;
   unit economics support meaningful CPCs once the new package AOV is validated.

## 9. Operations

- **Intake:** web form → document checklist email → case folder created.
- **Production:** standardized report templates per claim type; appraiser completes
  audit + valuation; QA pass against a methodology checklist; 48-hour SLA tracked
  from complete-documents receipt.
- **Delivery:** PDF report + evidence pack by email; Guided adds a 30-minute walkthrough
  and one factual addendum requested within 14 days.
- **Priority:** SLA starts only when the complete document set is received; refund the
  $49 add-on if 24-hour delivery is missed.
- **Tooling:** CRM (pipeline + email sequences), market data subscriptions
  (comparable listings, auction data, valuation guides), e-sign, payment processor,
  case management folders.
- **Compliance:** state appraiser licensing where required; clear
  not-a-law-firm/not-a-public-adjuster boundaries; Guided is document support only
  and excludes negotiation, insurer-response analysis, ongoing Q&A, representation,
  and scripts; attorney-reviewed terms,
  privacy policy, and guarantee language; E&O insurance.

## 12. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Insurers ignore reports | Escalation-ready format, appraisal-clause guidance, and transparent outcome data |
| Guided drifts into regulated advocacy | Fixed deliverables, no insurer contact, no response analysis, and attorney-reviewed boundaries |
| Priority demand exceeds capacity | Disable the Stripe add-on when the priority queue is full and refund any missed SLA |
| Specialty research creates scope creep | Required receipt/build-sheet intake and one defined specialty appendix |
| Guarantee abuse / refund drag | Free Snapshot pre-qualification; clear guarantee terms |
| Founder dependency | Documented methodology/templates from day one; second appraiser by month 9 |
| Valuation software vendors improve fairness | Specialty research and independent audit remain relevant; expand into adjacent ACV disputes |
| Seasonality/volume swings | B2B volume contracts smooth demand |

## Appendix: Reusing This Model in Other Niches

This site + plan structure is deliberately portable. To apply it to another
"independent claims advocacy" niche (homeowner insurance claims, medical bill
disputes, equipment valuations), swap:

- the **gatekeeper** (auto insurer → health system billing dept / property insurer),
- the **credentialed expert profile** (auto appraiser → licensed public adjuster /
  medical billing advocate / certified equipment appraiser),
- the **report chapters** (audit → independent valuation → correspondence pack
  generalize cleanly),
- the **free lead magnet** (offer/bill snapshot review), and
- the **tiered report, document-support, specialty-research, and priority-delivery** mechanics,

while keeping the same page architecture: fairness-led hero, founder story,
3-step process, tiered pricing with guarantee, results page with before/after
numbers, and a giving-back program tied to the niche's cause.
