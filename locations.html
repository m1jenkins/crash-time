Every state has its own total loss rules

Total loss thresholds, appraisal clause rights, and claim deadlines vary by state. Your state's laws shape the dispute, and our reports are built to work within them.

Total loss thresholds vary

Some states declare a total loss at 60% of actual cash value. Others wait until repair costs hit 100%. Several states use a formula that factors in salvage value. The threshold affects how much is at stake in your settlement.

Appraisal clause rights differ

A few states (like Texas and Washington) mandate an appraisal clause in every auto policy. In most states, the clause depends on your policy language. Either way, an independent appraisal report is the evidence that gives the clause teeth.

Deadlines cut both ways

Most states hold insurers to deadlines for acknowledging, deciding, and paying claims. Your own deadline matters too: statutes of limitations for filing range from one to six years depending on the state and claim type.
