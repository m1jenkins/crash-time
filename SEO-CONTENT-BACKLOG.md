## Editorial guardrails

- Do not claim that an insurer, CCC, Mitchell, Audatex, or an adjuster committed fraud, deception, or a “scam” without case-specific proof and legal review.
- Prefer precise language such as **low offer**, **unsupported adjustment**, **incorrect vehicle input**, **weak comparable**, or **valuation issue**.
- Explain that CCC and similar vendors produce valuation reports using insurer-submitted/configured information; the insurer makes the claim decision and offer.
- Separate first-party claims under the reader's own policy from third-party liability claims. Rights, deadlines, and escalation options can differ.
- Treat legal and financial statements as consequential content. Cite the current policy, Texas Department of Insurance, Texas statutes, or another primary source near the claim.
- Use a qualified human reviewer for state-law, appraisal, complaint, tax, title, and claims-practice content. Display the real review date.
- Never invent settlement results, average payout increases, screenshots, testimonials, credentials, or case studies.
- Do not create state, insurer, or valuation-vendor variants unless each page has unique evidence and standalone value.

## Detailed P1 content briefs

### TL-01: How to dispute a CCC ONE total-loss valuation report

- **Proposed URL:** `/dispute-ccc-one-total-loss-valuation.html`
- **Title direction:** How to Dispute a CCC ONE Total-Loss Valuation Report
- **H1 direction:** How to audit and dispute a CCC ONE valuation report
- **Primary need:** The reader has a CCC report and wants to know exactly what to check and how to respond.
- **Content promise:** A page-by-page audit that ends with a prioritized written correction request, not a generic instruction to “negotiate.”
- **Must-answer sections:**
  1. What CCC ONE does and who makes the settlement offer
  2. Get the complete report and identify its version/date
  3. Verify VIN, year, trim, drivetrain, mileage, packages, and options
  4. Audit every comparable: identity, listing status, geography, mileage, equipment, and seller type
  5. Recalculate and question condition, mileage, option, and other adjustments
  6. Separate correction requests from a disagreement over methodology/value
  7. Send a written dispute with an evidence index
  8. What to do if the insurer revises the report, refuses, or does not respond
  9. When a supervisor, independent appraisal, appraisal clause, or complaint may fit
- **Original assets:** Permission-cleared/anonymized CCC report; numbered annotation overlays; one-page audit checklist; before/after version comparison. Do not reproduce copyrighted material beyond what is permitted.
- **Primary sources:** Current CCC product/help documentation for what the platform represents; the reader's actual report/methodology disclosure; current TDI and Texas law for rights and escalation.
- **Internal links in:** Texas hub, ACV guide, master totaled-car guide, homepage guide section.
- **Internal links out:** TL-02, TL-03, TL-04, TL-05, counteroffer template, appraisal guide, free review.
- **Editorial risk:** Do not state that CCC intentionally undervalues cars. Attribute report inputs, decisions, and conclusions accurately.

### Evidence-first downloads worth building

1. **Valuation report audit checklist:** vehicle facts, comps, adjustments, math, settlement components, version/date.

### Insurer- and vendor-specific pages

Do not create programmatic pages such as “fight a GEICO/State Farm/Progressive/Allstate offer” from the same template. Otherwise, consolidate those queries into the CCC, Audatex, Mitchell, first-/third-party, or general report-audit guide.

### Specialty vehicle pages

Publish only when the service actually handles the vehicle type and the page can show a distinct valuation methodology. The EV, modified, classic, luxury, and work-truck pages need original examples and specialist review; they should not be cosmetic keyword swaps.

## Article quality checklist

Before drafting:

- Confirm the query has a distinct need from an existing canonical page.
- Review current search results and Search Console queries; record the observation date.
- Identify the page's one primary task and one primary CTA.
- Secure any real report, screenshot, case, or calculation needed to make the article original.
- List primary sources and assign a real subject-matter reviewer.

Before publishing:

- Answer the primary question near the beginning in plain language.
- Use one descriptive title and H1; write a unique snippet candidate.
- Cite consequential claims near the statement and show a substantive review date.
- Include at least one original asset, worked example, template, or expert analysis.
- Link to the hub, one adjacent guide, one relevant decision/service page, and the free review where natural.
- Add truthful `Article` and `BreadcrumbList` structured data matching visible content; do not add FAQ markup for hoped-for Google rich results.
- Check mobile layout, accessibility, canonical, rendered HTML, sitemap inclusion through the build process, and analytics events.

## Measurement and reprioritization

Establish a baseline and review by cluster, not by isolated keyword:

- Google Search Console impressions, clicks, average position, and query variants for each canonical page;
- indexation and canonical selection;
- visits from article to free review, sample report, pricing, and checkout;
- free-review starts and completed qualified submissions by landing page;
- recurring report errors/questions found in real leads;
- links or citations earned by templates, worksheets, and annotated examples;
- assisted conversions, not just last-click conversions.

After enough data accumulates, merge pages that attract the same need, strengthen pages earning impressions without useful engagement, and promote P2/P3 topics supported by real customer language. Do not promise rankings or a fixed traffic timeline.
