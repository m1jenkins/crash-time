How total loss claims work in Texas

Three things decide what you're paid after a Texas total loss: the actual cash value the insurer assigns your car, the evidence you bring to challenge it, and the dispute rights under Texas law.

Total Loss Threshold: 100%

In Texas, total loss threshold is set to 100%.

Your right to appraisal

Mandatory under SB 458 (Insurance Code Ch. 1813) for all personal auto policies issued or renewed on or after January 1, 2026. Either party can invoke appraisal to resolve a valuation dispute.

Deadlines & Regulations

Texas Prompt Payment of Claims Act (Ch. 542) sets specific deadlines for acknowledging, deciding, and paying claims. Missed deadlines can trigger 18% interest and attorney's fees. Governed under Transportation Code Ch. 501 (100% threshold), Insurance Code Ch. 542 (Prompt Payment), Ch. 1813 / SB 458 (Appraisal Clause) by the Texas Department of Insurance.

The ACV software works for them, not you

Cherry-picked comparables

Valuation software often anchors on the cheapest listings it can find - older trims, higher mileage, distressed sales - instead of what it actually costs to replace your car in your local Texas market today.

Unexplained "condition" deductions

Automated reports routinely mark vehicles down for "typical" wear that was never inspected, and miss options, packages, and recent maintenance that add real value.

Missing taxes and fees

Replacing a car isn't just the sticker price. Depending on your policy, applicable sales tax and title/registration fees belong in the conversation - and they're easy to leave off a fast settlement.

Texas total loss FAQs

When is a car considered a total loss in Texas?

In Texas, total loss threshold is set to 100%. In Texas, total loss threshold is set to 100%.

Can I dispute a total loss settlement offer in Texas?

Yes. You are not required to accept the insurer's first offer. You can present your own evidence of value - comparable local listings, options and condition documentation, and an independent vehicle value report.

Does Texas policies include an appraisal clause?

Mandatory under SB 458 (Insurance Code Ch. 1813) for all personal auto policies issued or renewed on or after January 1, 2026. Either party can invoke appraisal to resolve a valuation dispute.

Can I claim diminished value in Texas?

Available in third-party claims per TDI Bulletin B-0027-00. Subject to a two-year limitations period from date of loss.

How long does the insurer have to pay my claim in Texas?

Texas Prompt Payment of Claims Act (Ch. 542) sets specific deadlines for acknowledging, deciding, and paying claims. Missed deadlines can trigger 18% interest and attorney's fees.
