How total loss claims work in Georgia

Three things decide what you're paid after a Georgia total loss: the actual cash value the insurer assigns your car, the evidence you bring to challenge it, and the dispute rights under Georgia law.

Total Loss Threshold: TLF

Georgia uses the Total Loss Formula: a vehicle is a total loss when the cost of repair plus the salvage value equals or exceeds the actual cash value.

Your right to appraisal

Not mandated by state statute. Most Georgia auto insurance policies include an appraisal clause as standard contractual language. Check your policy declarations page.

Deadlines & Regulations

Georgia requires insurers to acknowledge claims within 15 days and approve or deny within a reasonable time. Bad faith refusal to pay can result in penalties plus attorney's fees. Governed under O.C.G.A. §33-4-6 (Penalty for insurer's refusal to pay claim), Georgia Unfair Claims Settlement Practices Act by the Georgia Office of Insurance and Safety Fire Commissioner.

Where an insurer valuation may need a closer look

Check the comparables

Confirm that each listing matches the year, trim, equipment, mileage, condition, title history, and realistic replacement area for your vehicle.

Review condition adjustments

Look for deductions that are not supported by an inspection, photos, or records, and check whether documented options, packages, and maintenance were included.

Confirm taxes and fees

Review the policy and settlement breakdown for applicable sales tax, title, registration, and other replacement-related items.

Georgia total loss FAQs

When is a car considered a total loss in Georgia?

In Georgia, total loss threshold is set to TLF. Georgia uses the Total Loss Formula: a vehicle is a total loss when the cost of repair plus the salvage value equals or exceeds the actual cash value.

Can I dispute a total loss settlement offer in Georgia?

Yes. You are not required to accept the insurer's first offer. You can present your own evidence of value - comparable local listings, options and condition documentation, and an independent vehicle value report.

Do Georgia policies include an appraisal clause?

Not mandated by state statute. Most Georgia auto insurance policies include an appraisal clause as standard contractual language. Check your policy declarations page.

Can I claim diminished value in Georgia?

Georgia is one of the strongest states for diminished value claims, available in both first-party and third-party claims under State Farm v. Mabry (2003).

How long does the insurer have to pay my claim in Georgia?

Georgia requires insurers to acknowledge claims within 15 days and approve or deny within a reasonable time. Bad faith refusal to pay can result in penalties plus attorney's fees.

This page is general information about Georgia claims, not legal advice. Your rights depend on your policy language, the facts of your claim, and current Georgia law.
