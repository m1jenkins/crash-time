How total loss claims work in California

Three things decide what you're paid after a California total loss: the actual cash value the insurer assigns your car, the evidence you bring to challenge it, and the dispute rights under California law.

Total Loss Threshold: TLF

California uses the Total Loss Formula: a vehicle is a total loss when the cost of repair plus the salvage value equals or exceeds the actual cash value.

Your right to appraisal

Not mandated by state statute. Whether your policy includes an appraisal clause depends on your insurer and policy form. California's Fair Claims Settlement Practices Regulations provide strong consumer protections for valuation disputes.

Deadlines & Regulations

California requires insurers to accept or deny claims within 40 days of receiving proof of claim. Payment is due within 30 days of settlement agreement. Governed under California Insurance Code §2695.8 (Fair Claims Settlement Practices), Cal. Code Regs. Title 10 §2695.8(b) by the California Department of Insurance.

California total loss FAQs

When is a car considered a total loss in California?

In California, total loss threshold is set to TLF. California uses the Total Loss Formula: a vehicle is a total loss when the cost of repair plus the salvage value equals or exceeds the actual cash value.

Can I dispute a total loss settlement offer in California?

Yes. You are not required to accept the insurer's first offer. You can present your own evidence of value - comparable local listings, options and condition documentation, and an independent vehicle value report.

Do California policies include an appraisal clause?

Not mandated by state statute. Whether your policy includes an appraisal clause depends on your insurer and policy form. California's Fair Claims Settlement Practices Regulations provide strong consumer protections for valuation disputes.

Can I claim diminished value in California?

Available in third-party claims. California courts have recognized diminished value as a recoverable damage in tort claims against the at-fault driver.

How long does the insurer have to pay my claim in California?

California requires insurers to accept or deny claims within 40 days of receiving proof of claim. Payment is due within 30 days of settlement agreement.
