Mileage math should move in the right direction.

Mileage is easy to display and easy to misread. Confirm the odometer on your car and every comparable, then follow each adjustment from the listed price to the adjusted value. If you cannot reproduce the logic, ask for the basis.

Audit My Mileage AdjustmentCheck the Comparables

By Mark West, Founder · Last reviewed July 21, 2026

Verify both sides of the comparison

Start with the loss vehicle. Check the report against an odometer photo, service invoice near the loss date, inspection record, or claim file. Then open each comparable listing and confirm its mileage. Dealer feeds, listing pages, and report summaries can disagree. Save the source and date.

If the loss happened between services, use the best dated record and explain any estimate. Do not quietly substitute today's mileage for the pre-loss reading.

Check the direction before the rate

Suppose your car had 40,000 miles and a comparable had 55,000. All else equal, the higher-mileage comparable needs an upward adjustment to estimate what it would indicate for your lower-mileage car. If the comparable had 25,000 miles, the adjustment would normally move the other way. This direction check catches many reading errors before you debate the size of the adjustment.

Do not assume one dollars-per-mile number

Mileage does not affect every vehicle at a fixed universal rate. The market reaction can change with vehicle age, segment, warranty status, mileage bands, and current supply. A ten-thousand-mile difference on a nearly new luxury vehicle may not behave like the same difference on a fifteen-year-old commuter car.

Ask whether the report applies a stated rate, a market-derived curve, or another method. If the method is not visible, ask the insurer for enough explanation to understand how the adjustment was calculated.

Look for consistency across the report

Compare how the report treats similar mileage gaps. Differences may be legitimate, especially when vehicles vary in age or equipment, but they should not be ignored. Also check that a later report version did not change an odometer or rate without explanation.

How to challenge the adjustment

Identify the exact mileage input or calculation you question. Attach the source that proves a corrected odometer. If the input is right but the method is unclear, ask for the calculation basis and a written explanation. Then tie the issue to the final adjusted value in your counteroffer.

Mileage cannot rescue a fundamentally weak match. Review the trim, drivetrain, condition, location, and listing status before treating a mileage-adjusted vehicle as sound.
